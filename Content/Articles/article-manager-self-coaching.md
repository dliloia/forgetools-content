## Summary
Most managers develop their teams but never develop themselves. Self-coaching is the practice of using your own patterns, behaviors, and feedback to continuously improve. This article explains how it works.

## Outline

### Introduction
You invest in developing your team. Who's investing in developing you?

### The Manager Blind Spot
Most people who become managers have never received management coaching
The 360 review is the exception, not the rule, and it happens once a year
Most managers go years operating on the same instincts without examination
The cost: patterns that don't serve you or your team, repeated indefinitely

### What Self-Coaching Is
Not self-criticism. Not just reflection.
Active examination of your own patterns with the intent to change them.
The discipline of treating yourself as you'd treat a direct report: with curiosity, specificity, and a growth plan.

### Your Patterns Are Showing
What your team sees that you may not
Common manager blind spots: canceling 1:1s, avoiding conflict, inconsistent feedback
How patterns compound over time
Why "I've always been this way" is not an excuse

### The Data You Already Have
Your 1:1 notes are a mirror
What topics come up repeatedly?
Whose development are you avoiding?
Which commitments do you routinely miss?
Whose progress is stuck?

### Questions That Drive Self-Coaching
Who am I avoiding having a hard conversation with, and why?
Which person on my team am I not developing, and what's my excuse?
Where am I still doing the individual contributor work instead of delegating?
What feedback have I received that I haven't acted on?
What would my team say I do when I'm stressed?

### The Weekly Practice
The 5-minute end-of-week manager reflection
What to capture, what to look for, what to do with it
How to build this into your 1:1 rhythm

### Getting External Input
Coaching vs. mentoring vs. peer learning
Why a thought partner accelerates what solo reflection can't
How to create informal feedback loops with your team
The skip-level as a self-coaching tool

### Using AI as a Coaching Layer
What ManagerForge's coaching features do
Pattern recognition across your 1:1 data
When AI coaching supplements vs. replaces human coaching

### The Self-Coaching Loop
Observe your own patterns
Name them honestly
Decide what you want to change
Create a specific behavior change
Evaluate the results
Start again

### Conclusion
The best managers are the most self-aware ones. That's not an accident. It's a practice.
