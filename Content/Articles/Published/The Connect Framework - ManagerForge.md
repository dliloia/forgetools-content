## Hook
Your 1:1 is not a status update. If you're using it that way, you're wasting your most powerful management tool.

## Body
Most 1:1s I've seen go like this:

"What are you working on?"
"Here's my update."
"Ok, here are my priorities for you."
"Anything else? No? Ok, see you next week."

That's not a 1:1. That's a check-in with a fancy name.

A real 1:1 looks different, I call it the Connect Framework:

**First 10 minutes:** Them, personally. How are they doing? What's going on outside of work that's affecting them? What's energizing them? What's frustrating them? You're learning who they are, not what they shipped.

**Next 10 minutes:** Their work. What's pressing? Where are they stuck? How can YOU remove blockers? This is where your job is to be useful to them — not to assign more tasks.

**Last 10 minutes:** Growth. Where do they want to go? What are they learning? What stretch would push them forward? What visibility can you create for them? Even if it's for a job someplace else, your job is to help them grow.

Notice what's missing: your agenda.

Your tasks, your priorities, your updates — those can happen any other time during the week. You have 40+ hours to manage. Give them 30 minutes that's actually theirs.

## CTA / Closing
That shift — from "my agenda" to "their time" — changes everything about the relationship. What does your current 1:1 structure look like? Be honest.
