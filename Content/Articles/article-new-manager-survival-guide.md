## Summary
Most new managers fail in the first 90 days by doing the wrong things. This guide covers the mental shift required, the common mistakes to avoid, and the concrete actions that set you up to lead well.

## Outline

### Introduction
Nobody told you it would be this hard. They probably didn't tell you much at all.

### The Mental Shift Nobody Prepares You For
From doer to developer
Your value is now measured through your team's output, not your own
The instinct to do the work yourself will undermine your team

### The First Two Weeks: Listen More Than You Talk
Why diagnosis comes before prescription
The listening tour: what to ask every person on your team
What you're listening for: trust, morale, clarity, capability, frustration

### Clarify Expectations With Your Manager
What does success look like at 30, 60, 90 days?
How does your manager want to be communicated with?
What decisions can you make vs. which require escalation?

### Set Up Your 1:1 Cadence
Start 1:1s in week one, every person, every week
Why this is the foundation of everything else
The CONNECT Framework as your structure

### Understand What Your Team Actually Does
Sit with people. Watch how they work. Ask questions.
Don't assume your experience as an IC transfers
The knowledge gap is real and temporary

### The Biggest Mistakes New Managers Make
Staying as an individual contributor
Giving feedback only through formal reviews
Avoiding hard conversations
Trying to be everyone's friend
Skipping 1:1s when things get busy

### Earning Trust Quickly
Consistency over charisma
Following through on every commitment, no matter how small
Being honest when you don't know something
Advocating for your team early and visibly

### The 90-Day Check-In
How to assess your own progress
What to ask your team
What to ask your manager
Adjusting your approach based on what you learn

### Building Habits That Compound
Why systems beat willpower for managers
The weekly rhythm that makes management sustainable
What to track, what to skip

### Conclusion
The first 90 days set the trajectory. You don't have to get everything right. You have to get the foundation right.
