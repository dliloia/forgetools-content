## Summary
Managing your relationship with your own manager is as important as managing your team. Most managers spend almost no time thinking about this. This article changes that.

## Outline

### Introduction
You spend a lot of time thinking about how to lead your team.
When did you last think seriously about how to manage your relationship with your own boss?

### You Are Somebody's Direct Report
The principles you apply downward apply upward
Why most managers ignore this half of the equation
The career cost of neglecting it

### What Your Manager Actually Needs From You
Not just updates. Value.
To not be surprised.
To be able to sponsor you.
Reliability they can count on.

### The 5 Habits of Managers Who Manage Up Well
Know your manager's top 3 priorities right now
Come to your 1:1 prepared with questions, not just updates
Flag problems early, before they become fires
Close every loop: if you said you'd do something, report back
Give them something real to advocate with

### The 1:1 With Your Manager
Most managers underprepare for this meeting
It's your meeting too, not just a check-in
How to run your side of it effectively
What to bring, what to ask, what to listen for

### Managing Up Without Being Fake
The difference between managing up and being a yes-person
Disagreeing productively and then committing
How to push back without damaging the relationship
Earning the right to challenge by demonstrating reliability first

### Understanding Your Manager as a Person
What are their pressures?
What do they care about most?
What frustrates them?
How do they prefer to communicate?

### When Your Manager Is Hard to Manage Up To
Difficult personalities
Managers who don't give feedback
Managers who micromanage
When to escalate vs. adapt vs. leave

### Conclusion
Your career is partly your manager's project. Give them the best possible version of you to work with.
