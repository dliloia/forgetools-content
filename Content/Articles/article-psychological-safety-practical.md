## Summary
Psychological safety is the most misunderstood concept in modern management. This article cuts through the vagueness and explains how it actually works and what you do to build it.

## Outline

### Introduction
"Psychological safety" has become a corporate buzzword. That's a shame, because the underlying concept is one of the most performance-relevant things a manager can create.

### What It Actually Is
Amy Edmondson's definition
The Google Project Aristotle findings
What it measures and what it predicts
Why it's a performance variable, not a feelings variable

### What It Is Not
It is not a guarantee of no conflict
It is not a requirement to like each other
It is not about being nice all the time
It is not the absence of accountability

### The Four Stages of Psychological Safety
Stage 1: Inclusion safety (I belong here)
Stage 2: Learner safety (I can ask questions and admit I don't know)
Stage 3: Contributor safety (I can offer ideas and challenge the status quo)
Stage 4: Challenger safety (I can push back on decisions, even leadership decisions)

Most teams get stuck between stages 2 and 3.

### What Destroys It
Reacting badly to bad news
Criticizing people in front of others
Saying you want honesty but punishing it when it arrives
Rewarding agreement and sidelining dissent
Not following through on what you say you'll do

### What Builds It
Being vulnerable first as the leader
Inviting disagreement explicitly
Responding well when someone challenges you
Thanking people for bad news as much as good news
Being consistent about what happens when someone speaks up

### The Manager as Model
Your team takes behavioral cues from you
How you react under pressure is the actual signal
The gap between what you say and how you behave
The compounding effect of a hundred small moments

### Building It in Practice
The questions that invite safety: "What am I missing?" "What aren't we saying?"
The retrospective as a structured safety practice

### Measuring It
Signs your team has it
Signs they don't
The signal in who speaks and who doesn't

### When Psychological Safety Goes Wrong
No accountability in the name of "safety"
Avoiding hard conversations as "being safe"
The difference between protection and avoidance

### Conclusion
You don't announce psychological safety. You build it, moment by moment, in how you respond when things are hard.
