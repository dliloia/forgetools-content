## Summary
Companies throw money at retention and keep losing great people. The research is clear on what actually works. This article tells the truth about retention and what managers can do about it.

## Outline

### Introduction
The most expensive thing a company can do is lose a great employee and have to replace them.
The most preventable thing a company fails to do is keep them.

### The Number Companies Get Wrong
Compensation matters. It's not the primary driver of voluntary turnover.
The data on why people leave: manager quality, growth, belonging, purpose
"People don't quit companies. They quit managers."

### What Managers Control (More Than They Think)
The research on manager impact on retention
What you can do regardless of what HR does or doesn't do
The leverage a single great manager has over an entire team's tenure

### What Actually Retains People
Career conversations. Not once a year. Ongoing.
Feedback that helps them grow. Not just evaluation.
Visibility and recognition. Knowing their work matters.
Advocacy. Someone in their corner.
Clarity. Knowing where they stand and what's expected.
Challenge. Interesting work that develops them.

### The Quiet Danger Signals
Signs someone is considering leaving
What disengagement looks like before the resignation letter
Why your highest performers are most at risk
How to have the conversation before it's too late

### The Stay Interview
What it is and how to run one
The questions that surface real answers
What to do with what you hear

### What Doesn't Work (And Why Companies Keep Doing It)
Retention bonuses (buy time, not loyalty)
Counteroffers (why they rarely work long-term)
Surveys that gather data nobody acts on
Perks and benefits without addressing the manager relationship

### The Career Development Conversation
How to have it effectively
What "I want to grow" actually means in different contexts
How to help people grow even when you can't promote them

### Building a Team That Stays
The culture a manager creates vs. the company's culture
Why teams with high turnover are usually manager problems in disguise
What sustainable tenure looks like and how you build it

### Conclusion
You can't keep everyone. But you can keep the right people, longer, with better outcomes, by being the kind of manager people don't want to leave.
