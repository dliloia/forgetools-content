## Summary
Most 1:1 meetings are wasted time. They turn into status updates that could have been an email. This guide covers the full philosophy, structure, and execution of 1:1s that build trust, develop people, and make you a better manager.

## Outline

### Introduction
The average manager runs 1:1 meetings every week and still doesn't know what they're for.

### What a 1:1 Is (And What It's Not)
- It is not a project status meeting
- It is not your agenda, it's theirs
- It is the primary tool for developing people
- It is where trust gets built or broken, week by week

### The Real Purpose of a 1:1
Three jobs this meeting does: relationship-building, coaching, development
Why all three require the manager to show up prepared and present

### The CONNECT Framework
The 10-10-10 structure:
- First 10 minutes: personal connection, what's going on in their life
- Second 10 minutes: their work, their challenges, what they need from you
- Third 10 minutes: growth, career, where they're headed

### Before the Meeting
How to prepare as a manager:
- Review your notes from last session
- Check what you committed to do
- Prepare 2-3 questions based on what you know about this person right now

### During the Meeting
Listening more than talking
Asking better questions
Taking real notes (and why this matters)
Following through on what you say you'll do

### After the Meeting
Documenting what happened
Flagging what needs follow-up
Building your evidence base for future reviews

### Common Mistakes and How to Fix Them
- Canceling too often
- Turning it into a status report
- Not taking notes
- Avoiding hard conversations
- Failing to follow through on commitments

### Making the Investment
Why 30 minutes per person per week compounds into career-defining relationships

### Conclusion
The 1:1 is the most powerful tool a manager has. Most managers don't use it. You can.
