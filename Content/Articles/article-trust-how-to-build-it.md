## Summary
Trust is not built in big moments. It is built through the boring, consistent daily behaviors that most managers underestimate. This article is the honest version of how trust actually works.

## Outline

### Introduction
Everyone says trust is important. Almost no one explains how it actually works.

### The Trust Ledger
Trust is an account with deposits and withdrawals
Every interaction moves the balance
Most managers don't realize they're overdrawn until someone quits

### What Builds Trust (The Actual List)
Showing up consistently. The 1:1 you cancel says something.
Doing what you say. The commitment you forget says something.
Being honest about hard things. The truth you soften says something.
Giving credit generously. The win you claim as yours says something.
Advocating when they're not in the room. The silence says something.

### What Destroys Trust
Managing by fear
Feedback only at review time
Claiming credit for team work
Being fake-positive about bad news
The gap between what you say and what you do

### Trust vs. Likeability
These are not the same thing
Managers who prioritize being liked often sacrifice trust
The most trusted managers sometimes deliver hard news
People don't need to like you. They need to trust you.

### The Trust Debt Problem
What happens when you've made too many withdrawals
Can trust be rebuilt? Yes, but it takes time
What rebuilding looks like in practice

### Building Trust With a New Team
The fastest paths to early trust
Why your first 90 days are disproportionately important
How to signal what you value without saying it explicitly

### Building Trust in a Remote Environment
The specific challenges of remote trust-building
What you have to compensate for when you can't be in person
How intentionality changes when proximity is gone

### Conclusion
Trust compounds. The deposits you make today are worth more than they look. Start now.
