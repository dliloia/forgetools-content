## Summary
Performance reviews are dreaded because managers don't build evidence all year. This article explains the system for making review season feel like assembly, not invention.

## Outline

### Introduction
It's review season. You're staring at a blank page. The problem started 11 months ago.

### Why Reviews Feel Hard (The Real Reason)
The blank page problem: managing by impression instead of documentation
Recency bias: the last two months are vivid, the beginning of the year is gone
The fairness problem: undocumented management isn't fair to employees

### The Fix Is Not the Template
Why better review forms don't solve the underlying problem
What actually solves it: ongoing documentation throughout the year

### The 26-Point Evidence Base
Each 1:1 is a data point
If you run 30-minute 1:1s weekly: 26 per person per year
What to capture: key topics, progress, concerns, commitments, growth moments

### What Good Notes Look Like
Not every detail, not a transcript
The signal: what would be meaningful to know in 6 months?
Examples of useful vs. useless documentation

### Turning Notes into Narratives
Quarterly check-ins vs. annual reviews
How to synthesize a year of notes into a performance story
The difference between impact and activity

### Writing the Actual Review
Starting with themes, not events
Specific examples as evidence
Calibrating to your organization's expectations
The conversation is more important than the document

### Having the Review Conversation
What to cover, in what order
How to deliver hard feedback in a review
How to make it forward-looking, not just backward-looking

### Tools and Systems
Why a dedicated tool beats a notes app
What ManagerForge does that a spreadsheet can't
Building the habit into your weekly rhythm

### Conclusion
Review season doesn't have to be dreaded. The work happens all year, in 30-minute increments.
