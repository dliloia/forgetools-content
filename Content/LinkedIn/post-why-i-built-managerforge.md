I built ManagerForge because I was tired of watching great people burn out under managers who never got better.

Twenty years in leadership. Five years at Amazon. Over 1,250 interviews as a certified Hiring Bar Raiser. I have watched more first-time managers fail at exactly the same things than I can count. Not because they weren't smart, and not because they didn't care. Because they had no system. They ran 1:1s with no agenda. They gave feedback only in reviews. They had no way to track what they'd promised or what their people actually needed. They managed by impression and intuition and hoped for the best.

When review season hit, they stared at blank pages, trying to reconstruct 12 months from memory. When a great person quit, they replayed the last few weeks wondering what they missed. I kept thinking there has to be a better way. Not a training course that gets forgotten in a month. Not a framework in a book that never gets applied. An actual tool, built into the rhythm of how you manage, that makes the discipline automatic and the patterns visible.

So I built it. ManagerForge captures your 1:1 notes, generates AI coaching based on your patterns, tracks what matters for each person on your team, and makes review season feel like assembly instead of guesswork. It's the tool I spent my career wishing existed. Every feature in it came from a real problem I either experienced myself or watched someone struggle with.

If you manage people and you're doing it without a system, try ManagerForge. First account is free, no onboarding call required.
