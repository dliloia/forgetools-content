## Hook
I became an Eagle Scout at 17.

I still run my team off three principles I learned then.

## Body
"Be Prepared" is the most famous Scout motto. But the one that changed how I lead is less famous:

Leave it better than you found it.

I got my Eagle rank by working with a team to build something lasting for a community I'd be leaving. That's it. That's leadership. Build something that serves people even after you're gone.

Three Eagle Scout principles that have never left me:

Prepare before you need to. In Scouts, you don't wait for rain to check your tent. In management, you don't wait for the performance review to give feedback. The whole point of a 1:1 is to catch things early, when you can still do something about them. Preparation is the job, not just the preliminary.

Serve because it matters, not because someone's watching. The best leaders I've known do the right thing when nobody's looking. They give the credit away. They take the blame. They show up for the team on hard days because that's the deal, not because it builds their reputation.

Leave every person better than you found them. Not every project. Not every quarter. Every person. If someone worked for you for two years and their career didn't grow and their skills didn't sharpen, that's a leadership failure. You are responsible for what you leave behind.

## CTA / Closing
Twenty-five years later I still think about what I'm leaving behind.

What's the most important thing you're building right now that will outlast you?
