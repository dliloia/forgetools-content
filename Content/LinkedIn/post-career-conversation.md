## Hook
The most important conversation you're not having with your team:

"Where do you want to go?"

## Body
Most managers never ask this.

Not explicitly. Not with real curiosity about the answer. Not in a way that invites honesty.

They're afraid of what they'll hear.

"I want your job." (Threatening.)
"I want to leave this department." (Uncomfortable.)
"I don't know." (Awkward.)
"I want to be promoted in 6 months." (How do I respond to that?)

So they don't ask. They manage the work, not the person.

And the employee eventually concludes: my manager doesn't care about my future.

Even if that's not true, the absence of the conversation IS the message.

Here's what I've learned in 20 years:

The managers who ask — and then actually help — keep their people longer, get more discretionary effort, and build the kind of loyalty that doesn't show up in engagement surveys.

You don't have to have all the answers. You just have to care enough to ask.

"Where do you want to be in 2 years?"
"What kind of work energizes you most?"
"What's the one thing I could do to help your career right now?"

That's it. Ask. Listen. Act on what you hear.

## CTA / Closing
The 1:1 growth segment — the last 10 minutes — is where this conversation lives.

When did you last ask your people where they want to go?
