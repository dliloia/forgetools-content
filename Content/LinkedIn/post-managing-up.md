## Hook
Most managers spend a lot of time thinking about how to lead their team.

Almost no time thinking about how to manage their relationship with their own manager.

That's a mistake.

## Body
You are somebody's direct report.

The same principles that make you a great manager down apply up.

Show up prepared. Don't just update — create value in the conversation. Know what matters to your manager and address it before they ask. Follow through on every commitment.

The managers who thrive long-term are the ones who understand their boss as a full human being with priorities, pressures, and goals — and figure out how to align with those.

That doesn't mean being a yes-person. It means being a trusted partner.

Specifically:

→ Know your manager's top 3 priorities right now
→ Come to your 1:1 with them with your questions ready, not just your updates
→ Flag problems early, not when they've become fires
→ Make it easy for them to sponsor you by giving them real results to talk about
→ Close the loop — always. If you said you'd do something, report back.

Managing up isn't politics. It's relationship management.

## CTA / Closing
Your 1:1 with your manager is as important as the 1:1s you run.

Are you treating it that way?
