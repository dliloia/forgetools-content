You are somebody's direct report.

Most managers spend a lot of time thinking about how to lead their team and almost no time thinking about how to manage their relationship with their own manager. That's a mistake. The same principles that make you a great manager downward apply upward. Show up prepared. Don't just update, create value in the conversation. Know what matters to your manager and address it before they ask. Follow through on every commitment. The managers who thrive long-term understand their boss as a full human being with priorities, pressures, and goals, and they figure out how to align with those.

That doesn't mean being a yes-person. It means being a trusted partner. Know your manager's top 3 priorities right now. Come to your 1:1 with them with your questions ready, not just your updates. Flag problems early, not when they've become fires. Make it easy for them to sponsor you by giving them real results to talk about. And close the loop, always. If you said you'd do something, report back. These aren't political moves. They're relationship management, and they're the exact same behaviors you want from your own direct reports.

The best managers I've known operated effectively in both directions. They invested just as much energy in the relationship with their manager as they did in the relationships with their team. That balance is what creates the kind of trust that leads to more autonomy, bigger opportunities, and real career momentum. Your 1:1 with your manager is as important as the 1:1s you run.

Are you treating it that way?
