## Hook
5 years inside Amazon taught me this about people:

The system doesn't develop you. Your manager does.

## Body
Amazon is famous for its intensity. The bar is genuinely high. The expectations are real.

Most people don't make it two years. I was there five.

And what I noticed — watching people thrive and watching people flame out — had very little to do with intelligence or work ethic. It had almost everything to do with their manager.

The people who thrived had managers who:
→ Gave them real feedback, not just performance ratings
→ Advocated for them in rooms they weren't in
→ Invested in their growth even when it was inconvenient
→ Were honest about what "good" looked like at Amazon specifically

The people who burned out often had managers who:
→ Led by fear
→ Gave feedback only in reviews
→ Took credit for their team's work
→ Were too busy surviving themselves to develop anyone else

Same company. Same system. Completely different outcomes.

The variable was the manager. It's always the manager.

I became a Bar Raiser partly because I wanted to make sure we were hiring managers who actually knew how to develop people. Not just execute projects.
