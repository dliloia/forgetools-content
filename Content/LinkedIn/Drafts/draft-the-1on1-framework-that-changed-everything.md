Your 1:1 should have zero items from your agenda.

I know that sounds extreme, so hear me out. The 1:1 is the one meeting that belongs entirely to your employee. You have every other meeting on the calendar to push your priorities. Staff meetings, project syncs, stand-ups, Slack. The 1:1 is theirs. I built a framework around this called CONNECT. It's a structured 30-minute format with three segments, and I've used it consistently for 20 years across every kind of team.

The first 10 minutes are personal connection. How are you doing? Not "how's the sprint," but how are YOU. What's going on in your life? What's on your mind? This is where trust gets built, and if you skip it, you're telling your employee you only care about their output. The second 10 minutes focus on their work, but not as a status update. What's challenging right now? Where are you stuck? What do you need from me? Your job in this segment is to listen and remove obstacles, not assign more work. The third 10 minutes are about growth. Where do you want to go? What skills are you developing? What would make this role more meaningful? This is the segment most managers skip entirely, and it's the one that drives retention more than anything else.

Here's what I've learned from using this consistently. When employees know the 1:1 is theirs, they show up differently. They prepare. They open up. They tell you things they'd never say in a group. They trust you faster because you've proven, every single week, that their time matters. The counterintuitive part is that removing your agenda makes you a more effective manager. When your team trusts you, they bring you problems earlier. You hear about risks sooner. You make better decisions because you have better information.

The 1:1 isn't a sacrifice of your time. It's the best investment you'll make all week. What does your current 1:1 structure look like?
