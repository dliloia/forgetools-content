## Hook
Trust isn't built in moments.

It's built in the boring consistency of showing up.

## Body
Every week, same time. Even when you're slammed.
Every promise, followed through on. Even the small ones.
Every hard truth, said out loud. Even when it's uncomfortable.

That's what builds trust.

Not the company offsite. Not the team happy hour. Not the motivational all-hands.

Those are nice. They don't move the needle.

What moves the needle is:

Did you show up to their 1:1 this week, or did you cancel it for the third time this month?

Did you tell them the truth about the reorg, or did you let them find out from someone else?

Did you follow through on that introduction you promised three weeks ago, or did you forget?

Trust is a ledger. Every consistent, reliable, honest behavior is a deposit. Every cancellation, vague non-answer, and forgotten promise is a withdrawal.

Most managers don't realize they're overdrawn until someone gives notice.

## CTA / Closing
The good news: the deposits are small and frequent. You don't have to be extraordinary. You just have to be consistent.

What does consistency look like in your 1:1s right now?
