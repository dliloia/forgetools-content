Trust isn't built in moments, it's built in boring consistency.

Every week, same time, even when you're slammed. Every promise, followed through on, even the small ones. Every hard truth, said out loud, even when it's uncomfortable. That's what builds trust. Not the company offsite, not the team happy hour, not the motivational all-hands. Those are nice, but they don't move the needle. What moves the needle is the stuff nobody sees because it's too mundane to photograph.

Did you show up to their 1:1 this week, or did you cancel it for the third time this month? Did you tell them the truth about the reorg, or did you let them find out from someone else? Did you follow through on that introduction you promised three weeks ago, or did you forget? Every one of these moments is a transaction. Trust is a ledger, and every consistent, reliable, honest behavior is a deposit. Every cancellation, vague non-answer, and forgotten promise is a withdrawal. The balance determines whether your people believe you when things get hard.

Most managers don't realize they're overdrawn until someone gives notice. That's because withdrawals happen silently. Nobody tells you they lost a little trust when you canceled the 1:1. Nobody flags it when you forgot the commitment. The erosion is invisible right up until it's catastrophic. But the deposits work the same way in reverse. Small, frequent, and compounding. You don't have to be extraordinary. You just have to be consistent.

The good news is that the bar is low. Show up. Follow through. Tell the truth. Do it every week. What does consistency look like in your 1:1s right now?
