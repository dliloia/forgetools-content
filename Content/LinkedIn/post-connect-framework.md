## Hook
The 30-minute 1:1 framework I've used for 20 years.

Three segments. Zero agenda items for the manager.

## Body
I call it the CONNECT Framework.

It's simple. It's structured. And it works at every level — from a first-time manager with 3 reports to a VP running a 200-person org.

**Segment 1 — 10 minutes: Them**
Not their work. Them.
How are they doing? What's good in their world right now? What's hard? What's on their mind that has nothing to do with the project they're on?

You're building a relationship. You can't lead someone you don't know.

**Segment 2 — 10 minutes: Their work**
What are they working on? What's pressing? Where are they stuck?
Your job in this segment is to remove obstacles, not add them. You're their blocker-remover. Nothing more.

**Segment 3 — 10 minutes: Growth**
Where do they want to go? What are they learning? What's the next stretch? What visibility can you create for them?

This is the segment most managers skip. It's also the one that drives retention more than anything else.

Notice what's not in any of these segments: your stuff.

Not your priorities. Not your updates. Not your ask-list.
That happens the rest of the week. You have time.

This 30 minutes? It's theirs.

## CTA / Closing
Try it for 4 weeks. Come back and tell me what changed.
