Three segments, zero agenda items for the manager.

I call it the CONNECT Framework. It's a structured 30-minute 1:1 format I've used for 20 years, and it works at every level, from a first-time manager with 3 reports to a VP running a 200-person org. The premise is simple: the 1:1 belongs entirely to your employee. You have every other meeting on the calendar to push your priorities. This one is theirs.

The first 10 minutes are about them as a person, not their work. How are they doing? What's good in their world? What's hard? What's on their mind that has nothing to do with the project they're on? You're building a relationship in this segment. You can't lead someone you don't know, and most managers skip right past the human connection because it feels unproductive. It's the most productive thing you'll do all week.

The second 10 minutes are about their work, but not as a status update. What's pressing? Where are they stuck? What obstacles can you remove? Your job in this segment is to be a blocker-remover, nothing more. You're not assigning new tasks or piling on priorities. You're listening for the things that are slowing them down and clearing the path so they can do their best work.

The third 10 minutes are about growth. Where do they want to go? What are they learning? What's the next stretch opportunity? What visibility can you create for them? This is the segment most managers skip entirely, and it's the one that drives retention more than anything else. When people feel invested in, they stay. When they feel like a cog, they leave. Notice what's not in any of these segments: your stuff. Not your priorities, not your updates, not your ask-list. That happens the rest of the week.

Try it for 4 weeks. Come back and tell me what changed.
