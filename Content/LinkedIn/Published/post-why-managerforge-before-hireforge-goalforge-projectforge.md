Your 1:1 quality predicts your hiring quality. Nobody talks about that connection.

Here's what I mean: a manager whose 1:1s are status updates has never practiced reading another person at depth, never built a working model of what makes their best people their best people. So when they sit across from a candidate, they're listening for the right words, not the right person.

That's one thread of a longer argument I've been building about why ManagerForge starts with 1:1s and not with hiring, goal-setting, or performance software. The order isn't a backlog, it's a thesis about where management actually breaks down.

If you've ever looked at a Lattice or a 15Five and felt like you were doing more paperwork than leading, this might explain why.
