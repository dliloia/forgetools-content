Your boss wants you to stay late. Not because there's work. Because of appearances.

A friend of mine starts early, finishes a full day of real work, and gets asked to stay late because his manager's boss shows up late and wants to see bodies at desks. That's not management. That's theater.

I've managed hundreds of people and conducted over 3,000 interviews. The performative stuff always costs you more than you think. Your best people notice it first, and they leave first.

The flip side: I once set up weekly 1:1s with four skip-level leaders. Three became some of the strongest professional relationships of my career. The fourth kept canceling. We never really connected. That contrast taught me something about the difference between face time and real time.
