Nobody writes about the middle of the founder grind. I'm going to.

I'm in it right now with ManagerForge. Product works. Paying customers. Good feedback. And a growth curve that looks like a horizon line. The trough is real, and "just keep building" doesn't fix it.

Here's the thing most technical founders miss when traction stalls: the product isn't the problem. The obscurity is. The honest move isn't to add features. It's to switch instruments entirely, from building to distributing, from your platform to other people's audiences.

I wrote about what that actually looks like, including a specific 7-day sprint, what it produces, and why being publicly bad at marketing is a prerequisite for getting good at it.
