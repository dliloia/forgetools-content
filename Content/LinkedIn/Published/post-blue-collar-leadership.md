Most leadership advice was written by people who never had to mop a floor.

I grew up watching my dad come home with grease under his fingernails. He never read a leadership book. But every person who worked with him respected him completely.

After 1,250+ interviews and 20+ years managing people, I can tell you: the best managers I've ever seen were never too important to get their hands dirty. The teams that run through walls were built by managers who led from earned authority, not credential.

Tech culture worships cleverness. But cleverness without grit creates fragility. The people who stabilize teams when things get hard understand that the whole job matters, not just the parts that look good.
