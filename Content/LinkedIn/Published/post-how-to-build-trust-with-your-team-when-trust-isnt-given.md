Most managers think trust is something you establish early. It isn't.

Trust is the cumulative result of small, repeated behaviors over months. The manager who shows up to every 1:1, follows through on what they said they'd do, and tells the truth when it's uncomfortable builds something that no amount of charisma or vision can substitute for. And the manager who cancels meetings, forgets commitments, and avoids hard conversations destroys it just as gradually, until one day the team has quietly checked out.

The asymmetry is what most people underestimate. Deposits are slow and small. Withdrawals are fast and large. A hundred kept promises builds real trust. One broken promise in a moment that matters can unwind months of it.

The most practical thing I've found: keep a short list after every 1:1 of what you committed to, and clear it before the next one. That single habit does more for trust than almost anything else.
