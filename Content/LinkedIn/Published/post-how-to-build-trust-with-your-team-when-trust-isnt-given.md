Most managers think trust is something you establish early. It isn't.

Trust is the cumulative result of small, repeated behaviors over months. The manager who shows up to every 1:1, follows through on what they said they would do, and tells the truth when it is uncomfortable builds something no amount of charisma can replace. The manager who cancels meetings, forgets commitments, and avoids hard conversations erodes it the same way: slowly, then all at once.

The part most people underestimate is the asymmetry. Deposits are slow. Withdrawals are fast. You can build trust for months and damage it in one moment that makes your team see you differently.

The most practical habit I know is simple: keep a short list after every 1:1 of what you committed to, and clear it before the next one. That one behavior tells your team your word means something.
