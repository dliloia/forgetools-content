Most feedback doesn't fail because it's wrong. It fails because it came six months too late.

When you save critical feedback for the annual review, you've already lost. The employee can't replay a moment from March when you're describing it in December. They can only take your word for it, and most people won't. The freshness window on feedback is short. Miss it, and you're just delivering a verdict with no right of appeal.

The other piece most managers miss: feedback is only as effective as the relationship it travels through. Technically perfect feedback lands like an attack when the trust isn't there. That's why your 1:1s matter more than you think.
