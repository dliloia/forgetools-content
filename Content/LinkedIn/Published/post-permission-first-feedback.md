Giving feedback without asking permission first is an ambush — even when your content is perfect.

Most managers know *what* they want to say. They've rehearsed it. The problem is choreography: they walk in, start talking, and the other person's defenses are up before the second sentence. Nothing lands. Nothing changes.

The fix is a five-move sequence that takes 60 to 90 seconds. It starts with two words: "Can I?" Asking permission shifts the listener from defense to receive. Once they say yes, the gate opens. You can walk through. Skip that step and you're delivering a well-constructed message to someone who's already locked the door.

The move most managers skip is step four: asking the other person to come up with the fix before you hand it to them. If they say "I don't know," hold the silence. Wait. It's uncomfortable, but filling it is a mistake. The actual answer usually comes in the next ten seconds.
