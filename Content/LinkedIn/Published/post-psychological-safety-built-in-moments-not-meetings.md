Psychological safety is not built in team meetings. It's built in the 60 seconds after someone brings you bad news.

Your engineer tells you the timeline you promised leadership is wrong. What you do next tells every person on your team whether speaking up is safe. React badly once, and they'll remember it for months. React well, consistently, and something starts to shift.

Google's Project Aristotle found psychological safety was the single biggest predictor of team performance. Not talent. Not compensation. Safety. Teams without it let small fires become disasters. Teams with it surface problems early and keep their best people longer.

The manager who says "my door is always open" and the manager who actually creates safety are often two completely different people. One is available. The other has earned it.
