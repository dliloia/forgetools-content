Why People Quit Managers (And What to Do Before They Do)

The exit interview is the most expensive feedback you will ever get.

By the time somebody is sitting across from you on their last day, the decision is already done, and the only thing the conversation produces is a confirmation of something that is six months in the past.

The real signal happens long before that, in five specific manager behaviors that show up over and over in the people who leave. Most of them are not the obvious ones, and most of them are fixable this week if you are paying attention.

If you have ever lost a high performer and quietly wondered if it was on you, the honest answer is probably more useful than you think.
