Most project plans are complete. The work inside them isn't.

I keep seeing the same pattern: a solid milestone roadmap, a capable team, and then two weeks in, half the people are blocked or quietly doing the wrong thing. The milestones looked like a plan. They weren't. Nobody ever translated the vision into actual, ownable tasks.

The gap between "launch beta to first 50 customers" and the specific work that makes it happen is where most execution falls apart. Closing that gap is a deliberate act, and it usually has to happen before the project starts, not after the first status meeting reveals the mess.

One thing I've learned the hard way: singular ownership matters more than most teams think. When two people share a task, both assume the other has it. Accountability dissolves quietly and nobody notices until something is late.
