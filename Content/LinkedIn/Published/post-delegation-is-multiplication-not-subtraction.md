Most managers treat delegation like giving something away. It's the opposite.

Here's the actual math: if you do a task yourself, the company gets one unit of output. Delegate it to someone at 50% of your skill level, and the company gets 1.5 units. You just multiplied output without adding headcount or hours. And that person doesn't stay at 50% forever.

The problem is that most managers won't let their people get the reps they need to improve. They step in when the work comes back at 70% of their standard, take it back, and feel good about protecting quality. What they actually did is guarantee that person never develops. And cap the team's output at one person forever.

There's a reason the best teams I've seen were built by managers who measured success by how often their people surpassed them.
