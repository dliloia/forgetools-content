Your organization already knows what keeps going wrong. Nobody is transmitting it.

That's the real pattern I've watched play out across every company I've been inside. After a painful failure, a leader sits in a retrospective and says "we need to make sure this never happens again." Meanwhile, someone two or three levels below them flagged the exact risk six months earlier, and it never traveled up the chain.

This isn't an information problem. The data exists. The lessons exist. The problem is transmission, and that's almost always a leadership failure, not a systems failure. Most managers are decent at pushing context down to their teams. They're terrible at pulling real signal back up. And when the chain between line-level reality and decision-makers breaks down, you don't get bad luck. You get the same problem, wearing slightly different clothes, every six to twelve months.

The fix is more deliberate than most leaders want it to be.
