A spotless track record isn't a green flag. It's a warning sign.

When someone has never made a mistake, or at least never admitted to one, only three things can be true. They haven't been tested hard enough for you to know how they perform under real pressure. They're coasting, calibrating their effort to avoid failure rather than achieve something. Or they're hiding, which is the most expensive possibility by a long shot.

The hidden mistake always costs more than the disclosed one. It compounds while you're not looking.

What I actually trust is the person who says "I don't remember doing it, but if I was the one working there, it must have been me," and then immediately asks what we're changing so it doesn't happen again. That response is rarer than it should be, and every time I see it, my trust goes up.
