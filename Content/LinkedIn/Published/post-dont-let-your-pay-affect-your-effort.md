Watched someone torch a great reputation over a raise they didn't get.

The frustrating part is how logical it feels in the moment. The company isn't treating you fairly, so you pull back. You're protecting yourself. What most people miss is that the person who made the compensation decision isn't watching your daily output. Your peers and your manager are, and they're drawing conclusions that have nothing to do with your pay dispute.

Your salary is confidential. Your effort is visible every single day to everyone around you. Those are two very different information streams, and conflating them is an expensive mistake.

If you're underpaid, address it directly, document it, and leave if the answer stays no. All of that is legitimate. What isn't is staying and coasting, because now you're underpaid and building a reputation as someone who checks out when things get hard. Both things are happening simultaneously.

Reputation compounds over a long career in ways that are easy to underestimate when you're frustrated about a paycheck.
