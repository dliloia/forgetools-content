If your team can't run for two weeks without you, you have a documentation problem.

Most managers frame this as a staffing issue or a skill issue. It's neither. The process lives in your head instead of somewhere your team can access, and that's a choice you're making every day you don't write it down.

The bar for documenting has dropped dramatically. Voice notes, transcripts, a ten-minute Loom walkthrough. What used to take an hour takes fifteen minutes now. The people still not doing it are no longer invisible. They're making a visible choice.

I wrote about the real cost of skipping this, the vacation test, the promotion test, and the one thing you can do today to start fixing it.
