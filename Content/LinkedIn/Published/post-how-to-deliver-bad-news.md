Most managers deliver bad news badly, and it has nothing to do with cruelty.

It comes from a simple, understandable instinct: they're managing their own discomfort instead of the person in front of them. So they bury the lead, over-explain the context, and soften the message until it's unrecognizable. The person leaves the meeting confused about what actually just happened. That's not kindness. That's a failure of the moment.

The fix is less intuitive than it sounds: lead with the news, then stop talking. One clear sentence, then silence. Every second of preamble before that sentence is a second the other person spends in dread, running through scenarios. You're not protecting them. You're prolonging it.

Preparation matters just as much as the delivery. Know your first two sentences cold. Know the logistics. Know what questions are coming and have real answers ready. If you're walking in without that, you're gambling with someone's livelihood and your own credibility.
