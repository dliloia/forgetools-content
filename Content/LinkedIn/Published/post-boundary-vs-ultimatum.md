Most people skip setting limits at work because the only version they've seen looks like a threat.

There's a real difference between a boundary and an ultimatum, and once you see it, you can't un-see it. A boundary describes what *you* will do. An ultimatum describes what *they* must do, or else. Only one of those is actually yours to control.

The confusion is why so many people do nothing at all. They see limit-setting as aggressive, so they go quiet instead. The behavior continues. And they slowly get comfortable tolerating the thing they resent.

There's a better way to set them, a reason the other person usually adjusts even when you didn't demand anything, and what happens when they don't. All of that is in the article.
