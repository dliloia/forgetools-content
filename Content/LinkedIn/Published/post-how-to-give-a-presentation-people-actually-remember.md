Most presentations are organized for the speaker's comfort, not the audience's understanding.

The structure, the slide order, the level of detail, all of it usually reflects what the presenter wanted to cover rather than what the audience needs to leave with. That's a subtle distinction but it explains why so many technically competent presentations don't actually land.

The fix starts before you open PowerPoint. If you can't say in one sentence what you want the audience to do, feel, or believe differently when it's over, you're not ready to build anything yet.

Strong delivery and clean slides matter, but they're downstream of that clarity. Get the outcome right first, and the rest of the decisions become a lot easier to make.
