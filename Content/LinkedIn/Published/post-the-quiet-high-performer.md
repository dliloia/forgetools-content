Your best employee isn't the one asking for more. That's the problem.

The person who delivers consistently, never complains, and never asks for anything is also the one most likely to resign without warning. By the time they say something, they've already made the decision.

Managers spend energy where there's friction. Underperformers, difficult personalities, loud dissatisfied employees. The quiet high performer never creates friction, so they never get attention. And eventually, they stop waiting to be seen.

One real conversation, not a status update, can change this. I wrote about what that conversation looks like and what's actually at stake when you don't have it.
