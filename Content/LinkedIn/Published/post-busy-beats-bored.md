You cut out all the after-work stuff. You felt worse, not better.

That's not a coincidence. Boredom and tiredness feel almost identical, but the interventions are opposites. Rest is the cure for fatigue. Engagement is the cure for boredom. If you apply the wrong one, you don't recover. You drift further out.

I spent a few days recently swapping everything I was building and learning for phone games and TV. Told myself I deserved the break. By day four I was auditing parts of my life that had nothing wrong with them.

The embarrassing root cause: I'd removed every source of voluntary engagement and replaced it with nothing. The system doesn't want empty space. It wants input.
