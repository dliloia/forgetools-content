The managers who get things done aren't the most senior ones in the room.

They're the ones who already have a relationship with the person they need. When something's blocked, they pick up the phone and sort it in ten minutes because they've been showing up consistently for months.

Cross-functional 1:1s with key peers, even just 15 minutes a month, are the most underused tool I see managers skip. Not because they're hard. Because they feel awkward to set up when there's no obvious agenda.

There's a simple message that works, a structure that makes the conversation useful, and a way to figure out which peer relationships to prioritize first. I wrote all of it out.
