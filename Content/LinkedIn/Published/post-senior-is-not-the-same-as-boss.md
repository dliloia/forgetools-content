Most junior employees are deferring to people who have zero authority over them.

They've been doing it since day one. In school, the older person was always the authority, teacher, coach, senior student. That template worked for 18 years, so they carry it straight into the workplace. Anyone with more tenure gets treated like a boss-equivalent.

Most of the time, those people cannot fire you, won't write your review, and have no formal power over your career at all. The worst thing they can do if you push back calmly is be briefly annoyed.

"I'm in the middle of something, can I find you in ten minutes?" is a normal thing to say to a peer. You can say it to a senior colleague too. Knowing that changes a lot.
