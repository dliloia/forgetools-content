Most managers develop their best people in private. Then they wonder why leadership doesn't see what they see.

I've been in thousands of calibration meetings over 33 years. If your high-potential person is unknown to the senior leaders in that room, they are at a disadvantage before a single word is spoken. Visibility is a strategy you execute deliberately, months before any opportunity opens up.

The other thing most managers skip is the honest promotion conversation. Not the vague "you're doing great" version. The real one: here's exactly what ready looks like, here are the two or three things I need to see, and here's what I'm doing to advocate for you in the rooms you're not in.

The managers who build the best teams aren't the ones who hire the best. They're the ones who grow people relentlessly and advocate for them loudly. That reputation compounds.
