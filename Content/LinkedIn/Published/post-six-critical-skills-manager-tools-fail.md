You're using six different tools to do one job. That's not working.

Strip management down to what actually matters and you land on six skills: running real 1:1s, hiring well, setting goals that stick, managing through projects, giving feedback in real time, and coaching yourself. Every major management tool covers one or two of these well and leaves the rest to you.

Lattice owns performance reviews. Greenhouse owns hiring. Asana owns projects. None of them know about each other, and none of them know about you as a whole manager carrying all six at once.

The integration cost is real, and nobody's counting it. I wrote about why the six-skill framing matters, and what to do with the one skill you've been avoiding looking at.
