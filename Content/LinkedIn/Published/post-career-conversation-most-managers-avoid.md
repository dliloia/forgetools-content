Most managers know what their people are working on. Very few know where they want to go.

There's a reason for that. Asking opens a door, and managers are afraid of what's on the other side. What if someone wants a promotion you can't give? What if they want a role that doesn't exist on your team? So the question never gets asked. The employee notices, draws their own conclusion, and starts quietly disengaging.

When a manager doesn't ask, employees don't assume neutrality. They assume indifference. That assumption is the beginning of a resignation you won't see coming.

I wrote about the five questions that open a real career conversation, including how to have it when you can't promise a single thing. Worth a read before your next one-on-one.
