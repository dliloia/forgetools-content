What you tolerate, you cultivate. Most managers learn this too late.

The first time you let something go, it feels like a reasonable call. Maybe the deadline wasn't that critical. Maybe you were picking your battles. But that first exception becomes the new baseline, and the team doesn't announce it. They just quietly reorganize their behavior around what they now understand the real standards to be.

By the time the pattern is visible enough to address, you're not having a conversation about one missed deadline. You're unwinding a cultural norm that's been in place long enough that people built their whole workflow around it. That conversation is ten times harder than the one you could've had in month one.

The thing worth asking yourself right now: what has your team stopped raising because nothing happened the last few times they did?
