Most managers are running their 1:1s completely wrong. And they don't know it.

The default 1:1 is a status update dressed up as a relationship. The manager walks in with their agenda, works through their list, and calls it a check-in. The employee sits across from them wondering why they even have this meeting.

There's a better structure. It takes 30 minutes, splits into three segments, and has one rule that most managers will immediately resist: your agenda is not allowed in the room.

That rule alone changes everything about how your team sees you.
