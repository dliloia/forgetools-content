Nobody taught you how to manage people. They just gave you a team and wished you luck.

You got promoted because you were great at your job. Then one day you were responsible for other people's careers and performance, with no training, no playbook, and no one telling you what "good" actually looks like.

So you figured it out the hard way. On real people. With real consequences.

I've been that manager. 20+ years of leadership, 5 years at Amazon as a Bar Raiser, 1,250+ interviews. The same story repeats everywhere: the best individual contributors become managers, they don't get trained, and their teams feel it.

The article covers what I wish someone had told me on day one.
