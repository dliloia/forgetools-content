## Hook
Nobody taught you how to manage people.

They just gave you a team and wished you luck.

## Body
You got promoted because you were great at your job.

Then one day you were suddenly responsible for other people's careers, performance, and wellbeing,  with no training, no playbook, and no one telling you what "good" actually looks like.

So you figured it out the hard way.

On real people. With real consequences.

I've been that manager. And in 20+ years of leadership, including 5 years at Amazon as a Bar Raiser, conducting over 1,250 interviews,  I've seen the same story repeat everywhere.

The best individual contributors become managers. They don't get trained. They default to what they know.

And their teams feel it.

Here's what I wish someone had told me on day one:

- Your job is no longer to just do the work. It's to develop the people who do.
- The 1:1 is your most powerful tool. Most managers waste it.
- Trust is built in boring consistency, not grand gestures.
- Your employees don't need a friend. They need someone who shows up, tells the truth, and cares about their growth.

That's it. That's the job.
