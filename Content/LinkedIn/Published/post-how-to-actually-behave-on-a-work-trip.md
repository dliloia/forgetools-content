Work trips are quiet career evaluations nobody warns you about.

Most people prep for the meeting and completely ignore everything around it. The breakfast, the Uber, the hallway conversation before the dinner starts. Those unstructured moments are where people actually form opinions about you, and those opinions tend to stick longer than any presentation you'll give.

The detail I keep coming back to: follow-through after you land matters more than most people realize. A specific, personal note to someone who gave you their time does more work than almost any in-meeting impression you made. Most people skip it because the trip is over and they're back in their inbox. That gap is an opening.

If you're the most senior person on the trip, the standard is even higher. The table takes its cues from you, the junior folks are watching how you handle logistics and downtime, and your behavior sets the ceiling for what the whole trip looks like culturally.
