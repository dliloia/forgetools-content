Most managers can explain AI. Almost none have changed how they work.

The ones getting real value aren't the ones who attended the most webinars. They're the ones who opened the tool on a random Tuesday with an actual problem, figured out what it could do, and built a small habit around that. No initiative required, no IT approval needed.

The highest-value use I've found for managers specifically is preparing for difficult conversations. Give the model the shape of the situation, what tends to go wrong, what outcome you need. It will find holes in your approach faster than most colleagues will, because it has no social stake in telling you the comfortable version.

The risk worth naming: some managers are using these tools to skip the thinking rather than sharpen it. Pasting a performance review in and signing whatever comes back is not using AI well. Your people can tell the difference between a manager who thought carefully about their development and one who didn't.
