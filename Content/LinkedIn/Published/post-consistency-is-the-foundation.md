The most difficult manager I ever had taught me more than almost anyone else I've worked with.

He was cold, blunt, and hard to read at first. I butted heads with him for months. But he was consistent, every interaction had the same shape, same standards, same priorities. Once I figured out the pattern, I stopped wasting energy reading the room and started doing real work. That relationship became one of the most productive of my career.

The problem was never him. The problem was that I hadn't adapted yet. His consistency gave me the raw material I needed to do that.

Most leadership writing treats consistency as a soft virtue and moves on. It's not soft. It's the structural load-bearing wall underneath every working relationship you have as a manager. And most managers are quietly eroding it without knowing it.
