You can't improve what you can't see. Most leaders are operating blind.

The problem isn't that they're lazy or careless. It's that they're running on feel. The leader has a general sense of how things are going, the team seems busy, work appears to be getting done. That feeling becomes the status report.

Then something fails. Everyone is surprised. And the painful part is it didn't have to be.

There's a specific word for what fixes this: mechanism. Not a metric, not a process. A mechanism is the practice that produces measurable signal on a schedule, closes the loop, and turns a number into a decision. The new article breaks down what that actually looks like in practice, including the cheapest mechanisms a small team can install this week.
