I thought I was about to get fired. I probably deserved to be.

Six months into my Amazon tenure, a $20k vendor project blew up in testing. The engineer had known partway through that my requirements wouldn't work, and built them anyway. Fixing it would cost $40k more. I spent the night writing a six-page paper about exactly what I had done wrong and why, and the next morning I sat in a glass cube while my boss and his boss read it in silence.

Twenty silent minutes is a long time when you're waiting to find out if your job still exists. At the end, my boss's boss looked up and said, "You just got through your first big screw-up at Amazon, and you handled it well."

That moment changed what I believe accountability actually means, and the lesson isn't what I expected.
