Most managers think they're running 1:1s. They're holding status meetings with a friendlier name.

After managing hundreds of people, I've seen the same failure modes wreck 1:1s repeatedly. Turning them into status updates. Canceling when things get busy. Doing all the talking. Zero follow-through on what was raised. Asking "how's everything going?" and accepting "good" as an answer.

Here's the part that stings: a manager running bad 1:1s for one year with a team of six wastes 300+ hours of combined time. But the real cost is what never happens. The problems that don't surface. The trust that's never built. The resignation you never saw coming.

The 1:1 is the highest-leverage 30 minutes a manager has each week. The article breaks down all seven failure modes and how to fix them.
