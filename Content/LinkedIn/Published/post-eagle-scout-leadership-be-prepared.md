Most managers wait for problems to find them. The best have already solved those problems before they arrive.

I earned my Eagle Scout rank at sixteen. Didn't think much about it for twenty years. Then I became a manager, built teams, and watched careers flourish and crash. Suddenly three years in the woods started making a lot of sense.

Every elite manager I've ever worked with lives by the same three principles, whether they wore the uniform or not. They boil down to three questions: Am I prepared for the problems that haven't arrived yet? Am I serving my people, or serving my own comfort? Is my team better than it was ninety days ago?

Simple questions. Hard to consistently answer yes. The managers who do stand out in every room they walk into.
