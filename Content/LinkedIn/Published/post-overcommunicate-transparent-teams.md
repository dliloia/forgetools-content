Your team doesn't hate the pivot. They hate that nobody explained it.

I've watched good engineers start quietly updating their resumes after a project got scrapped. Not because the decision was wrong. Because nobody took ten minutes to explain why.

When work gets thrown away without context, people fill in the blank themselves. Usually with "our work didn't matter." That resentment builds quietly until someone accepts the competing offer. The fix isn't complicated. It's explaining your reasoning before you announce the outcome.

Five minutes of transparency prevents months of disengagement. I've seen it work hundreds of times.
