Not taking vacation isn't dedication. It's stealing from your company.

Most leaders think skipping PTO means more contribution. The math says otherwise. Running at 90% for six months costs your organization twelve full days of output. A week of vacation costs five. You're not in the black by grinding through. You're in a hole.

The degradation is the problem. It's invisible, it's gradual, and your 85% starts to feel like your normal. You forget what full capacity felt like.

If you lead a team, this isn't just about you. Your people watch what you model. When you skip vacation, they skip vacation. Now multiply that loss across everyone who reports to you.
