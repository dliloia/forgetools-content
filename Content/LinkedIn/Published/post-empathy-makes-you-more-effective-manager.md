Empathy keeps getting taught like a personality trait. It's a skill.

The managers I've seen hold the highest standards over time tend to have the strongest team relationships. That's not a coincidence. When people trust that you actually understand their context, hard feedback lands differently. They're not spending energy wondering if you misread the situation. They can receive it and act on it.

There's also an information gap worth taking seriously. Your team has already built a pretty accurate model of you. You probably haven't built the same model of them, and the organizational structure makes that asymmetry the default. People manage up. They smooth out the rough edges before the update reaches you. Understanding how someone thinks and what they're afraid of is what starts closing that gap.

The practical version of this is simpler than most trainings make it: ask questions that go one level deeper than status, listen long enough that people finish their actual thought, and remember the things they told you last time. Consistent, not complicated.
