Silence isn't neutrality. Leadership fills the void with whatever story fits.

Most managers assume good work speaks for itself. It rarely does. Senior leaders are carrying enormous cognitive load, and your team's progress is not their priority to track. That job falls to you.

The shift worth making is from reporting to communicating. Status updates tell leadership what happened. Vision communication tells them where you're going and why it matters for something they care about. Those are genuinely different conversations, and only one of them builds the kind of investment that protects your team when decisions get hard.

If you can describe your team's direction in two clear sentences and connect it to a business outcome, you have everything you need. The rest is consistency: finding the moments, doing the translation, and making the story repeatable enough that someone else can tell it in a room you're not in.
