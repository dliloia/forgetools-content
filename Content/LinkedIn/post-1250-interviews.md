## Hook
I conducted 1,250+ interviews at Amazon.

Here's the one thing that separated great managers from everyone else.

## Body
As a certified Amazon Bar Raiser, my job was to evaluate candidates across the entire organization — not just my own team. Engineers, PMs, marketers, ops leads, VPs. Thousands of hours of interviews over 5 years.

And one thing predicted management quality more reliably than anything else:

**How they talked about their people.**

Great managers told stories where other people were the heroes.

"My team built this." "She figured out the solution." "He pushed back on my idea and he was right."

Weak managers told stories where they were always the hero.

"I saved the project." "I made the call." "I pushed them to do it."

That's it. That's the tell.

The best managers I've ever met saw their job as creating the conditions for other people to succeed. The rest saw their job as succeeding through people.

The difference sounds small. The outcomes aren't.

## CTA / Closing
If you want to know what kind of manager you are: listen to how you describe your team's wins.

Who's the hero of your stories?
