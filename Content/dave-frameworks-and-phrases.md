# Dave's Frameworks and Phrases

A growing catalog of the named ideas and signature phrases that surface in Dave's day-to-day management. Each entry includes what it means, when it shows up, and what content it has seeded so the content engine doesn't accidentally repeat itself.

When a new phrase or framework comes up in a meeting, transcript, or recording, add it here with a date and source. Phrases compound over time. The ones that keep recurring are the ones that belong in the book.

---

## Signature Phrases

### "What you tolerate, you cultivate."
**Meaning:** Whatever behavior a manager lets slide becomes the new floor. Culture is the sum of what you have decided not to interrupt.
**When it shows up:** Performance drift, late-arriving accountability, "how did we get here" culture conversations.
**Linked ideas:** `Content/Ideas/what-you-tolerate-you-cultivate.md`
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14 (used twice in same 1:1 about handling workplace interruptions).

### "Adapt or die."
**Meaning:** Resourcefulness compounds; specific skills go obsolete. The single most important career asset is the ability to learn and adjust.
**When it shows up:** Career conversations, talking about why long tenure at one job is risky, AI displacement questions.
**Linked ideas:** `Content/Ideas/adapt-or-die.md`
**First catalogued:** previously.

### "The boundary is for you, not for them."
**Meaning:** A boundary describes what you will do; an ultimatum demands what someone else must do. Boundaries hold even when the other person ignores them, because the action is yours.
**When it shows up:** Workplace assertiveness coaching, especially with junior employees who confuse "saying no" with "issuing demands."
**Linked ideas:** `Content/Ideas/boundary-vs-ultimatum.md`
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14.

### "Communication at work is one of the hardest things in the world."
**Meaning:** Workplace communication is a learned skill, not a personality trait. Most managers never get formal training on it, and the people who appear naturally good at it have usually been quietly practicing for decades.
**When it shows up:** Coaching young employees through their first hard conversations.
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14.

### "You act the way you want others to act, and people start to adapt."
**Meaning:** Culture changes when you model the behavior, not when you announce it. Subordinates mirror peers; peers mirror leaders; leaders mirror each other.
**When it shows up:** Toxic-team conversations, new-manager onboarding, "how do I fix this culture" questions.
**Linked ideas:** see `Content/Ideas/Making culture real not a poster on the wall.md`
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14.

### "Just because they're senior doesn't mean they're your boss."
**Meaning:** Junior employees often defer to senior coworkers as if they have authority over them, when they don't. Power and tenure are different things. Reasonable behavior is reasonable to ask for at any level.
**When it shows up:** Coaching early-career employees through interactions with tenured peers.
**Linked ideas:** `Content/Ideas/senior-is-not-the-same-as-boss.md`
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14.

### "Not training someone is irresponsible."
**Meaning:** When a company hires someone and then expects them to figure the job out by watching, it is shifting a managerial duty onto the new hire. Documentation, recordings, and written instructions are part of the manager's job, not optional polish.
**When it shows up:** Conversations about onboarding, knowledge transfer, and the "I had to learn it myself, so they can too" trap.
**Linked ideas:** `Content/Ideas/not-training-is-irresponsible.md`
**First catalogued:** 2026-05-14.

## Named Frameworks

### The CONNECT Framework
A 30-minute 1:1 structure split into three 10-minute segments: personal connection, work, growth. Built so the 1:1 belongs entirely to the employee.
**Status:** Published. See `Content/Articles/Published/The Connect Framework - ManagerForge.md`.

### Permission-First Feedback
Dave's conversational choreography for delivering feedback in a way that actually changes behavior. Five moves: ask permission, describe behavior not person, name the impact and outcome, invite them to solve, confirm and thank.
**Status:** Idea captured. See `Content/Ideas/permission-first-feedback.md`. Article-worthy.

### The Unit of You
Capacity model where a fully rested person is 1.0. Skipping vacation drops the unit; degradation accelerates non-linearly. Used in the "Not Taking Vacation Is Stealing From Your Company" article.
**Status:** Published in the Vacation article (2026-04-29).

## How to Add Entries

When a new phrase, framework, or recurring metaphor shows up:

1. Add it to the appropriate section above. Use the same structure: meaning, when it shows up, linked ideas, first catalogued date.
2. If it's a coined phrase Dave repeats, file it under Signature Phrases. If it's a named system with multiple parts, file it under Named Frameworks.
3. If there is no corresponding idea file yet, create one in `Content/Ideas/` and link it.
4. Keep the meaning line short. The catalog is a hub, not a writeup.

Phrases that show up once might be throwaway. Phrases that show up across two or three different conversations belong in the book.
