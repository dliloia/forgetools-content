## The Idea
A complete management tool would cover hiring, 1:1s, goals, projects, performance reviews, coaching. Most management software companies build one of those and call it complete. The bigger ones (Lattice, 15Five) try to do all of them at once and end up shallow at each.

The bet behind ManagerForge is that 1:1s are the keystone skill. If your 1:1 is broken, your hiring instincts are blunt, your goal-setting is performative, your project oversight is reactive, and your performance reviews are a surprise. If your 1:1 is sharp, all of those downstream surfaces get easier. Fix the keystone first, then build outward.

## Why it matters
This is positioning content, not a how-to. It explains *why* the product line is sequenced the way it is — the order is a thesis, not a backlog. It also previews the eventual HireForge, GoalForge, ProjectForge expansion in a way that doesn't promise dates. Buyers who get the keystone idea will wait for the rest. Buyers who don't will leave for a deeper Lattice or a shallower Notion template, and that's fine.

## Tone notes
Confident. Opinionated. Make the keystone argument concrete with a real example: a manager whose 1:1s are status updates will set vague goals, miss hiring tells, surprise people in performance reviews. A manager whose 1:1s are real will catch all of those upstream.
