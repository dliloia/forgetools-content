Riley joined your team four weeks ago. They came with a strong resume and interviewed well. You were excited about the hire.

But things aren't going smoothly. Riley's first two deliverables needed significant rework. They seem hesitant to ask questions in team meetings, though they've been asking their desk neighbor for help constantly. In your last 1:1, Riley said they were "doing fine" but their body language told a different story.

You've also noticed that your onboarding documentation is outdated. The last person who joined the team was eight months ago, and several processes have changed since then. Other team members have made a few comments to you like "Riley's been asking me a lot of basic questions" and "I think they're in over their head."

Your next 1:1 with Riley is this afternoon.

**Consider:**
- What's your responsibility in Riley's slow start versus what falls on Riley?
- How do you have an honest conversation that's supportive without being dishonest?
- What concrete steps do you put in place for the next 30 days?
- At what point would you escalate your concerns, and to whom?
