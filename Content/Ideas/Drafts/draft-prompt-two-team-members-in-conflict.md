  Strong response includes: talking to each person separately first to understand both perspectives; focusing on behaviors and impact rather than character judgments; not taking sides or playing favorites; setting clear expectations for professional behavior regardless of personal feelings; facilitating a direct conversation between the two people rather than acting as a permanent go-between; addressing the systemic issue (unclear ownership, competing priorities) rather than just the interpersonal symptoms; following up after resolution.

You manage a team of six. Two of them, Priya and Marcus, have been increasingly tense with each other over the past three weeks. It started with a disagreement about how to handle a technical decision on a shared project. Now it's spreading.

In your last team meeting, Priya made a sarcastic comment about Marcus's approach to testing. Marcus responded by going quiet for the rest of the meeting. Two other team members have separately mentioned the tension to you. One said, "It's getting uncomfortable."

You haven't addressed it directly yet. Both Priya and Marcus are strong performers individually, but the collaboration has broken down. Pull requests are getting passive-aggressive comments. Decisions that should take a day are taking a week because neither will agree.

**Consider:**
- Do you address this in 1:1s first, or bring both people together?
- How do you understand the root cause without playing therapist?
- What specific expectations do you set for how they work together going forward?
- How do you protect the rest of the team from the fallout?
