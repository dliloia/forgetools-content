evaluation_rubric: |
  Strong response includes: addressing it directly rather than letting it slide because of their track record; approaching with curiosity first ("what happened?") rather than assumptions; recognizing that high performers missing deadlines is often a signal of something deeper (burnout, overcommitment, unclear priorities, personal issues); holding the same standard of accountability regardless of past performance; framing the conversation as concern for them, not just the deliverable; adjusting workload or support if the root cause warrants it; not over-correcting by micromanaging someone who normally operates independently.
  Weak response misses: ignores it because "they earned a pass"; addresses it but only superficially ("just don't let it happen again"); assumes the worst without asking questions; uses the moment to pile on other minor grievances; creates anxiety by overreacting to a single miss; treats it identically to how they'd treat a consistent underperformer missing a deadline.

Sam has been on your team for 18 months and has been consistently your top performer. They deliver ahead of schedule, mentor junior team members, and rarely need oversight. You've told them multiple times that you trust them more than anyone on the team.

Last Friday, Sam missed a client-facing deadline for the first time. The deliverable was two days late. The client noticed and escalated to your director, who asked you what happened. You didn't have a good answer because Sam hadn't flagged any issues in your 1:1 the week before.

When you look at Sam's recent work, you notice they've taken on two extra projects in the last month, including one that was supposed to go to a teammate who was out sick. Your next 1:1 with Sam is tomorrow.

**Consider:**
- How do you open this conversation?
- What's the balance between accountability and understanding?
- How do you address the pattern of overcommitment you're now seeing?
- What changes, if any, do you make to how you manage Sam going forward?
