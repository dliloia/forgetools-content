Most managers walk into 1:1s cold. They glance at the calendar, see the name, and wing it. "So... what's on your mind?"

That's lazy. And your direct reports can tell.

Five minutes of prep transforms a 1:1 from a check-in to a coaching conversation. Here's what to review before you sit down:

Your notes from last time. What did they say they were working on? What commitments did you make? Did anything come up that you should follow up on?

Their recent work. Any big deliverables, presentations, or milestones since your last conversation? Anything you noticed, good or bad?

The bigger picture. Are they on track toward the goals you've set together? Is there a career conversation you've been putting off? Any team dynamics you should surface?

That's it. Five minutes. You can do it while walking to the meeting room or waiting for the Zoom to connect.

When you show up prepared, people notice. They feel like the meeting matters to you, not just to them. And the quality of the conversation goes up dramatically because you're not spending the first ten minutes trying to remember where you left off.

Here's a trick: set a recurring five-minute calendar block right before each 1:1. Use that time to review your notes and write down one or two things you want to bring up. Walking in with even one intentional question changes the entire dynamic.

Your people prepare for their 1:1s with you. Return the favor.
