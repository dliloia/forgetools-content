evaluation_rubric: |
  Strong response includes: acknowledging if the review should not have been a surprise (and taking ownership if it is); being direct about the rating without excessive hedging or sandwich-method padding; providing specific examples tied to clear expectations that were set in advance; separating the person's value from their recent performance; presenting a clear improvement plan with measurable goals and a timeline; committing to more frequent check-ins and support; being honest about consequences while leaving room for genuine improvement; managing their own discomfort rather than rushing through the conversation.
  Weak response misses: buries the negative rating in compliments so the person doesn't realize how serious it is; delivers the review without specific examples; hasn't documented performance issues throughout the review period; makes it feel punitive rather than developmental; doesn't provide a path forward; avoids discussing consequences; reads from a script without engaging in real dialogue; gets emotional or overly apologetic in a way that makes the employee feel they need to comfort the manager.

It's annual review time. You need to deliver a "needs improvement" rating to Casey, a mid-level project manager on your team. Over the past six months, Casey has struggled with stakeholder communication, missed several internal deadlines, and received feedback from two cross-functional partners about lack of follow-through.

You've addressed some of these issues in 1:1s along the way, but you're not confident you were direct enough each time. Looking back at your notes, you gave hints and suggestions rather than clear "this needs to change" messages. Casey may not fully understand the severity of where things stand.

Casey is generally well-liked on the team and puts in long hours. The issues aren't about effort or attitude. They're about execution and communication at the level the role requires.

Your review meeting is scheduled for tomorrow morning. You have the written review ready, but you haven't delivered it yet.

**Consider:**
- How do you structure the conversation from start to finish?
- How do you handle it if Casey becomes emotional or defensive?
- What do you own about how you managed the situation leading up to this review?
- What support do you offer going forward, and what timeline do you set?
