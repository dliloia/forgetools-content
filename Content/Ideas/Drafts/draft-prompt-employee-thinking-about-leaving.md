You're in your regular Thursday 1:1 with Jordan, one of your strongest engineers. The meeting has been normal so far. Status updates, a quick chat about an upcoming deadline. Then, near the end, Jordan says:

"I want to be honest with you. I've been thinking about whether this is still the right place for me. I've started talking to a couple of other companies."

Jordan has been on your team for two years. They're reliable, well-respected by peers, and consistently deliver high-quality work. You had no idea this was coming. There were no obvious warning signs.

You have about 30 minutes left in your day, and Jordan is looking at you, waiting for a response.

**Consider:**
- What do you say first?
- How do you explore what's driving this without making assumptions?
- What do you do after the meeting ends?
- How do you balance honesty about what you can and can't change with your desire to retain them?
