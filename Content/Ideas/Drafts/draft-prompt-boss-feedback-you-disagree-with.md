evaluation_rubric: |
  Strong response includes: pausing before reacting and asking clarifying questions ("Can you give me a specific example?"); separating the delivery of the feedback from its content; genuinely considering that they might have a blind spot; seeking additional data points by asking peers or direct reports; acknowledging the feedback without necessarily agreeing immediately; creating a plan to test whether the feedback is accurate; recognizing the power dynamic and managing the relationship thoughtfully; being honest about the emotional difficulty of receiving critical feedback.
  Weak response misses: immediately dismisses the feedback as inaccurate or unfair; gets visibly defensive or argues in the moment; agrees superficially to end the conversation with no intention of reflecting; assumes malicious intent from their boss; doesn't seek additional perspectives; treats the situation as a political problem to manage rather than a growth opportunity to explore; fails to recognize that disagreement with feedback doesn't mean the feedback is wrong.

You just finished your quarterly check-in with your boss, Dana. Most of the conversation was positive. Your team's output is strong, you hit your targets, and a recent project launch went well.

Then Dana said: "One thing I want you to work on. I've heard from a couple of people that you can come across as dismissive in cross-functional meetings. Specifically, when other teams propose ideas, you tend to shut them down quickly instead of exploring them."

This doesn't match your self-image at all. You see yourself as direct and efficient. You push back on bad ideas because you're trying to protect your team's bandwidth. You've always considered this a strength.

But Dana is telling you it's landing differently than you intend. You don't know who gave this feedback. You're not sure you agree with it. And you're a little stung.

**Consider:**
- How do you respond to Dana in this moment?
- What do you do with this feedback over the next week?
- How do you investigate whether there's truth to it without becoming paranoid?
- If it turns out the feedback is accurate, what do you change?
