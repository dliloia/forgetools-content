You manage a team of five. Four of them are solid, reliable contributors. The fifth, Morgan, has been increasingly inconsistent over the past two months.

Specifically: Morgan regularly misses self-imposed deadlines, produces work that requires more review cycles than anyone else on the team, and has been absent from two team meetings without explanation. When you ask about status in 1:1s, Morgan says things are "on track" but the output tells a different story.

The rest of the team has noticed. Your most senior team member pulled you aside last week and said, "I don't want to throw anyone under the bus, but I've picked up three of Morgan's tasks this month and I'm getting burned out." Another person made a pointed comment in standup about "some people" not updating the project board.

You like Morgan personally. They were a strong contributor in their first year. Something has shifted, but you're not sure what.

**Consider:**
- How do you prepare for your next conversation with Morgan?
- What do you say, and what do you specifically avoid saying?
- How do you address the team's frustration without throwing Morgan under the bus?
- What's your plan if the first conversation doesn't lead to improvement?
