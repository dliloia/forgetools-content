## The Idea
If you've been doing consistent, documented 1:1s all year, the performance review is not a painful exercise — it's a summary of work you've already done. The 1:1 is the insurance policy against the blank-page panic that hits every manager in review season.

## Why it matters
Most managers dread review season because they've been managing by impression, not documentation. They try to remember what happened six months ago and end up writing vague, useless reviews. The fix isn't a better template — it's consistent 1:1s with good notes, all year.

## Possible angles
- Before/after: the manager who documented vs. the manager who didn't
- The math: 26 weekly 1:1s with notes vs. 0 notes at review time
- What a review looks like when it's built from real data vs. vibes
- The employee's perspective: what it feels like to receive a review with specific examples vs. generalities
- How to retrofit this approach if you're already behind
