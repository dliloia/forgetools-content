## The Idea
New managers make predictable mistakes — not because they're bad, but because nobody told them what the job actually requires. The transition from individual contributor to manager is one of the hardest in professional life, and it almost never comes with real preparation.

## Why it matters
The mistakes new managers make in the first 90 days often define their team's culture for years. Getting this right early is vastly more valuable than fixing it later.

## Possible angles
- Mistake 1: Still doing the individual contributor work
- Mistake 2: Trying to be friends with everyone
- Mistake 3: Avoiding hard conversations until they become crises
- Mistake 4: Not listening enough in the first 30 days
- Mistake 5: Managing everyone the same way
