## The Idea
Most founder content is written either right before launch ("here's what I'm building!") or after exit ("here's how I scaled to a million users!"). Almost nothing is written from inside the trough — the part where the build is real, the product works, the metrics are flat, and the temptation is to either build more product or quit. That trough is the actual job.

The honest move when traction stalls isn't usually to add features. It's to switch instruments: go from building to distributing. From product to story. From your tool to other people's audiences. The metric that matters is not how good your tool is, it's how many people know it exists.

## Why it matters
Every solo founder lives in this stall at some point. Most fail because they respond to it by building more product (which produces nothing they didn't already have) instead of by getting in front of more humans (which produces what they actually need: feedback, signups, motivation). Naming the trap is half the answer.

## Tone notes
This is candid founder content, not a how-to. Dave should write from the actual middle of his own ManagerForge experience: real numbers, real frustration, real distance between his head and the dashboard. Don't sanitize.

## Possible angles
- The exact moment you realize "I've been polishing the product for weeks and nothing has moved"
- Why "build more" feels like progress and isn't
- The discomfort of distribution work for technical founders
- A specific 7-day distribution sprint (Product Hunt, IH, HN, newsletter swap, LinkedIn) and what it actually produces
- The case for being publicly bad at marketing on the way to being good at it
