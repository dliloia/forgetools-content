## The Idea
Most feedback doesn't work — not because the content is wrong, but because of how and when it's delivered. Feedback given in the moment, with specificity, in a relationship of trust, lands. Feedback dumped in a performance review six months after the fact does nothing.

## Why it matters
Managers avoid feedback because they're afraid of the reaction. But the absence of feedback is its own message: that the manager doesn't care, doesn't notice, or doesn't trust the employee enough to be honest. Real development requires real feedback.

## Possible angles
- The SBI model (Situation-Behavior-Impact) with real examples
- Why the annual review is the worst time to deliver important feedback
- How to build the relationship that makes hard feedback land instead of sting
- The difference between feedback and criticism (and why employees can feel it)
- How to use the 1:1 to create a continuous feedback loop
