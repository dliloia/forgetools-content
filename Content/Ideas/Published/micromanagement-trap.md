## The Idea
Most micromanagers don't think of themselves as micromanagers. They think of themselves as thorough, high-standards, invested managers who care about quality. The behavior is the same; the self-perception is completely different. Understanding the mechanics of how good managers slide into micromanagement is the first step to avoiding it.

## Why it matters
Micromanagement destroys autonomy, which destroys engagement, which destroys performance. It's a slow-motion team killer. And it's usually triggered by stress — when deadlines tighten or stakes rise, the instinct is to control more. The 1:1 is the antidote: when you have real visibility through conversation, you don't need to hover.
