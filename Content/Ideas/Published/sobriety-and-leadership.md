## The Idea
Dave has been sober since 2012. The skills recovery requires — radical honesty, asking for help, leading from vulnerability, owning mistakes without excuse — are exactly the skills that make someone an exceptional leader. The connection is not coincidental.

## Why it matters
Most leadership development programs teach people to project confidence and hide vulnerability. Recovery teaches the opposite. And the leaders who can admit uncertainty, own failures, and ask for help are the ones teams trust completely — because they're the only ones who are actually telling the truth.

## Possible angles
- What sobriety taught Dave about honesty in leadership
- Leading from vulnerability vs. performing strength
- How asking for help builds authority instead of undermining it
- The 1:1 as a space where vulnerability is safe
- Why Dave includes his sobriety in his professional bio — and why that choice matters
