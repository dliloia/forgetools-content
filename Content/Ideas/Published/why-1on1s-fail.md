## The Idea
The most common 1:1 mistakes: using it as a status update, canceling when busy, letting the manager drive the agenda, no follow-through on action items, no structure, treating it as optional.

## Why it matters
Managers think they're doing 1:1s. They're not. They're having check-in meetings and calling them 1:1s. The distinction matters enormously. A real 1:1 builds trust and surfaces problems early. A fake 1:1 burns the employee's time and signals that the manager doesn't really care.
