## The Idea

Work should be a meritocracy. Those who work hardest and smartest should succeed. Being asked to stay late for appearances, not output, is performative and harmful.

## Source Material

Dave's friend is a salaried employee who starts early and puts in a full day. His boss asks him to stay later because HIS boss shows up later in the day. The goal isn't productivity. It's keeping up appearances.

Key points from Dave:
- If the ask is performative, it's bullshit
- Not about whether you work 8 hours or 12. It's about whether the hours have merit and add value
- Being asked to put in "face time" with leaders so they like you is focusing on the wrong thing
- The goal should be building a REAL relationship, not a facade

## Connected Idea: Skip-Level 1:1s Build Real Relationships

Dave's example: at a recent role, he set up weekly 1:1s with four skip-level leaders.

- Three became phenomenal relationships. Even after leaving the org, he stays in touch with them.
- Some started slightly contentious because they disagreed philosophically. But over time, getting to know each other personally, understanding intentions, and working collaboratively built a strong bond.
- The fourth leader canceled almost every week. They met maybe once every two months. That relationship never developed.

Lesson: the regular meeting is what builds the relationship. Not showing up performatively, but consistently investing real time.

Connects back to the Connect Framework and why skip-level 1:1s matter.

## Possible Angles

- "Your boss wants you to stay late. Here is why that is the wrong ask."
- "Face time is not the same as real time"
- "I set up 1:1s with four leaders above me. Three became lifelong allies. The fourth kept canceling."
- "Stop performing. Start connecting."
