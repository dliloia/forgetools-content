## The Idea
Cross-functional 1:1s with peers in other departments are one of the most underused leadership tools. The manager who invests in peer relationships before they need something gets dramatically better outcomes than the one who only reaches out when there's a fire.

## Why it matters
Your ability to get things done in any organization depends heavily on your peer relationships. People help the people they know and trust. Regular 1:1s with key cross-functional partners — even monthly — build the kind of relationship capital that makes everything easier.

## Possible angles
- Why most managers only talk to peers when there's a problem
- How to structure a peer 1:1 (different from a direct report 1:1)
- The ROI of 15 minutes/month with a key stakeholder
- How to identify which peer relationships matter most
- What to say to set up your first peer 1:1 without it being weird
