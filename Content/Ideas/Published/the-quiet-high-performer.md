## The Idea
Every team has one — the person who does great work, never complains, never asks for anything. And because they never ask, they never get. They're also the most likely to leave without warning, because by the time they say something, they've already decided.

## Why it matters
Managers are conditioned to spend energy on problems — the underperformer, the difficult personality, the loud dissatisfied employee. The quiet high performer gets the least attention and carries the most risk. One conversation every two weeks can prevent losing someone irreplaceable.

## Possible angles
- How to identify the quiet high performer on your team right now
- Why they don't ask for what they need (and what they need anyway)
- The specific 1:1 questions that unlock what's really going on
- Signs that a quiet high performer is about to leave
- The development investment that keeps them engaged: stretch assignments, exposure, visibility
