## The Idea
Most people have heard of the 80/20 rule. Very few people actually use it. The principle, also called Pareto analysis, is that 80% of your problems are caused by 20% of the underlying issues. The corollary that nobody quotes is also true: 20% of your problems are caused by the other 80% of the underlying issues.

The application that matters is not the platitude. It is the analysis. Categorize the actual problems hitting your team or your customers, count occurrences, weight by impact (dollars, customer pain, hours lost, whatever fits the situation), and rank. What you almost always find is that a small handful of issues account for most of the pain. Those are the things to fix this quarter. Everything else can wait.

This stops being a productivity cliche and starts being a roadmap tool the moment you actually do the analysis.

## Why it matters
Most product roadmaps are negotiation outputs, not analysis outputs. They reflect what the loudest stakeholders asked for, not what the data says will move the needle. A Pareto analysis is one of the cheapest ways to convert that negotiation into a real prioritization conversation, and it works whether you are running a product team, an ops team, or a single client engagement.
