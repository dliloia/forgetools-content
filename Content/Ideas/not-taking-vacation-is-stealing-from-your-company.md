## The Idea

When you don't take vacation, your performance erodes over time. The cumulative loss to your company exceeds the cost of taking the time off. Not taking vacation is stealing output from your organization.

## The Unit of Dave Framework

Dave at full capacity = 1 unit of Dave. Over time without rest:

- Performance erodes to 0.9, then 0.75, and the further down the scale, the faster it drops
- At 90% (optimistic estimate): you lose 10% every day
- In a 20-day work month: the company loses 2 full days of output
- Over 6 months: 12 lost days
- One week of vacation costs 5 days but returns you to 1.0
- Net savings over 6 months: ~7 days of recovered output

The math: the company loses MORE from sustained reduced performance than from a week of vacation.

Some people are at 75% or 50% because they're so burned out. The numbers get dramatically worse.

## Why This Is Controversial (And Why It Shouldn't Be)

People think: "If I don't take vacation, I work more and contribute more." Factually true in the short term. Devastatingly wrong over time.

A bad night's sleep affects performance. What happens after a year without real rest?
