## The Idea
Most managers never explicitly ask their people: where do you want to go? What does success look like for you in 3 years? What kind of work energizes you? They're afraid of opening a door they can't close, or making a promise they can't keep. So the conversation never happens. And the employee assumes their manager doesn't care — because the evidence suggests they don't.

## Why it matters
Employees who believe their manager is invested in their career stay longer, work harder, and recommend their manager to others. The career conversation is a direct driver of retention. Avoiding it is a slow leak in the relationship.
