## The Idea
A management title doesn't come with trust. Trust is built through consistent behavior over time — showing up when you said you would, following through on what you promised, telling the truth when it's inconvenient. The 1:1 is where trust is built or destroyed week by week.

## Why it matters
Every management problem traces back to trust. Low trust teams underperform, over-communicate defensively, hoard information, and churn. High trust teams move fast, take risks, tell you problems early, and stay. The investment in building trust through consistent 1:1s pays every dividend.

## Possible angles
- The trust account: how every 1:1 is a deposit or withdrawal
- What breaks trust fastest (hint: canceled 1:1s, unkept promises, vague feedback)
- How long it takes to build trust vs. how fast it can be destroyed
- Trust signals that employees actually watch for
- The consistency advantage: why boring reliability outperforms charisma
