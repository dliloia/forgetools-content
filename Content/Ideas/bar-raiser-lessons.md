## The Idea
As an Amazon Bar Raiser, Dave evaluated candidates across every function. The patterns he saw in what separated good managers from great ones — and how those patterns showed up in interviews — is some of the most specific management insight available.

## Why it matters
Most management advice is generic. This isn't. It comes from thousands of hours evaluating real humans in high-stakes situations, and from watching how the best managers thought about their work when no one was watching.

## Possible angles
- The single question that revealed whether someone was a real manager or just a title-holder
- What the Bar Raiser role taught Dave about what companies say they want vs. what they actually select for
- How Amazon's leadership principles map to real management behavior (not the PR version)
- The pattern Dave saw in every great people manager in those 1,250 interviews
- How to self-evaluate your own management approach using Bar Raiser criteria
