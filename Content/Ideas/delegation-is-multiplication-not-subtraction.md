## The Idea

When you don't delegate, the maximum output your company gets is 1 unit of you. When you delegate to someone at 50% of your skill level, the company gets 1.5 units. Delegation is multiplication, not subtraction.

## The Potter Story

Two groups of potters (Dave notes he's not sure if the story is true, but the lesson is):

- Group 1: Focus on making only perfect bowls. Take as much time as needed. Don't move on until the bowl is perfect.
- Group 2: Iterate as fast as possible. Make as many bowls as you can.

Same time limit. Results:
- Group 1: small number of gorgeous, perfect bowls
- Group 2: enormous number of terrible bowls, BUT the number of GOOD bowls far exceeded Group 1

Why: more chances to try, fail, learn, and succeed. Iteration beats perfectionism.

## Application to Management

1. **If your team isn't up to the challenge, they never will be unless you give them the chance.** The only way people get good at tasks is through experience. The only way to get good experience is through trial and error.

2. **There is only one of you.** Without delegation, max output = 1.0 you. With a 50% employee: output = 1.5 you. As you invest in them, they improve. Eventually they might exceed you.

3. **Every manager should hope their reports surpass them.** If your employee becomes better than you at the task, you become a fractional unit of them. That is success.

4. **Holding work tight to your chest robs the company** of: more output, faster task completion, your ability to focus on strategy.
