## The Idea
The data is clear and has been for decades: the #1 reason people leave jobs is their manager — not pay, not benefits, not the company. The manager is the variable. Yet companies keep investing in employer branding instead of manager development.

## Why it matters
Every manager who loses a high performer has to ask the hard question: was it me? Often the honest answer is yes — not because they were malicious, but because they were underprepared. Understanding why people quit managers is the first step to becoming one people stay for.

## Possible angles
- The data on manager-driven turnover (real numbers)
- The 5 specific manager behaviors that predict departure
- What employees say in exit interviews vs. what they actually mean
- The cost of turnover per employee (calculable, eye-opening)
- How consistent 1:1s reduce turnover risk by building the relationship before the crisis
